How this works

Bleacher Breeze translates current weather at Wrigley Field into public-facing batted-ball estimates. The baseline is a neutral day: 70°F, 50% relative humidity, 29.92 inHg pressure, and 0 mph wind.

Average fly ball impact

The main Carry card starts with a 3.75 ft/mph physics baseline for peak home-run trajectories, then applies a 0.35 airflow attenuation factor. This blends the estimate down for ordinary high-air contact, including pop-ups, line drives, and deep flies.

Max HR potential

The smaller line under Carry shows the unattenuated wind effect for a better-struck home-run trajectory. Headwinds receive a 1.2x drag modifier because they tend to knock balls down harder than tailwinds help them.

Air Density

Air Density uses temperature, relative humidity, and surface pressure. Warmer, lower-pressure, more humid air generally helps carry. Colder, higher-pressure air generally suppresses carry.

The wind vector is projected against Wrigley’s center-field orientation, so pure crosswinds do not falsely show as outward carry. Side Drift uses a smaller 0.75 ft/mph crosswind factor. These are directional estimates, not Statcast-grade projections.
