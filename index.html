How this works

Bleacher Breeze translates current weather at Wrigley Field into a simple batted-ball estimate. The baseline is a neutral day: 70°F, 50% relative humidity, 29.92 inHg pressure, and 0 mph wind.

Carry

Outward or inward wind is estimated at 3.75 feet per 1 mph of wind component. The app scales that slightly toward 4.0 ft/mph in lighter air and toward 3.5 ft/mph in heavier air.

Side drift

Crosswind is estimated at 0.75 feet per 1 mph of crosswind component, shown as left-to-right or right-to-left movement.

Air Density

Air Density uses temperature, relative humidity, and surface pressure. Warmer, lower-pressure, more humid air generally helps carry. Colder, higher-pressure air generally suppresses carry.

These are directional estimates for a typical home-run trajectory, roughly 100 mph exit velocity and a 28° launch angle. Real batted balls vary by launch angle, spin, exit velocity, and exact wind patterns inside the park.
